King of the Court Part 1

Year 1

With the 1st overall pick of the 2003 NBA draft the Cleveland Cavaliers drafted LeBron James out of St. Vincent-St. Mary High School to turn their franchise around after finishing the previous season with a record of 17-65. Dwyane Wade, and Chris Bosh were also members of this draft class this will become very important later.

LeBron James debuts with a scorching hot 25 points 9 assists and 6 rebounds against the Sacramento Kings. LeBron was showing early that he deserved the hype and was ready to play in the NBA straight out of high school.

Locker room turmoil emerges. Vets such as Ricky Davis were unhappy with the new direction of the team and LeBron's immediate emergence as a star player. He famously said "I thought LeBron James was just going to be another addition to help me score." Ricky Davis was traded mid season to the Boston Celtics

On March 27th 2004 LeBron becomes the youngest player to score 40+ points in a win over the New Jersey Nets

The Cavaliers missed the playoffs by 1 game. One of the biggest storylines of this season was who would win Rookie of the Year between LeBron and Carmelo Anthony but ultimately LeBron won the award.

Year 2

The Cavaliers fired their head coach mid season. They finished the season with a record of 42-40 narrowly missing the playoffs. LeBron took a huge leap this season finishing with All NBA second team honors. New owner Dan Gilbert decided to clean house with the front office and coaching staff to prepare for next season. The biggest addition was hiring Mike Brown as head coach.

LeBron once again made the all star team and would do so every for the rest of his career from this point. On March 20, 2005, LeBron dropped 56 points against Toronto, which was the highest scoring game by a player in franchise history.

Lebron showed massive improvement starting off the season hot averaging 26 points, 6 assists, and 7 rebounds along with 1 block and 2 steals per game in the first month of the season. The Cavaliers were 9-5 throughout the first month.

LeBron joins team USA for the 2004 Olympics. They end up finishing with a bronze medal with LeBron coming off the bench later calling the experience a "waste of time".

The 2004 off season had alot of shakeup. Carlos Boozer a huge impact player who would later make all star teams departed in free agency to the Utah Jazz. 2 notable players the Cavaliers traded for were Anderson Varejao and Drew Gooden from the Orlando Magic. Gooden was an excellent rebounder and brought scoring impact, Varejao recently drafted by the Magic was a defensive minded hustle player.

Year 3

To kickoff the 2005 off season, new owner Dan Gilbert gave all star center Zydrunas Ilgauskas a contract extension. Then Dan Gilbert decided to go all in on free agency signing Larry Hughes to a huge contract who was coming off a big year averaging 22 points and leading the league in steals. Donyell Marshall a stretch four, and lastly Damon Jones.

Early on, the all in off season acquisition of Larry Hughes looked like it was paying off. The Cavaliers had a statement win in late December over Pistons one of the defining teams of the era. However, just a few days later Larry Hughes broke his finger and needed to have surgery so he was sidelined for the rest of the season.

After the injury the Cavaliers started to plummet but ended up rallying late in the season to finish with a record of 50-32 securing the 4 seed with LeBron playing heavy minutes to keep the team afloat. LeBron finished the year getting 2nd place in MVP voting.

In the first round of the playoffs the Cavaliers matched up against the Washington Wizards. The Cavaliers ended up winning the series in 6 games. Round 2 however, they played against the juggernaut Detroit Pistons coming off of 2 straight trips to the NBA finals and a championship 2 years prior. The Cavaliers took them to 7 games but ultimately lost the series.

Year 4

Unfortunately for the Cavaliers their run came to an end when the matched up against the Spurs dynasty led by Tim Duncan. One of the defining teams of the era. An elite defensive unit that shut down the driving lanes for LeBron and dared him to shoot which at the time was one of the weaknesses in his game. Without a real number 2 scoring option to take pressure off of LeBron the Cavaliers were swept in the first round.

In the first round they swept the Wizards. In the second round they played against the Nets and beat them decisively in 6 games. Their biggest test though came in the conference finals where they once again matched up against the Pistons the the same team that eliminated them the previous season. In a hard fought series the Cavaliers won in 7 games to advance to the NBA finals.

The Cavaliers ultimately finished the season with the exact same record as the previous season going 50 - 32 however this time around that was good enough for the 2 seed in the Eastern Conference.

By the all star break and the trade deadline they were on pace to finish with a similar record to the previous season. The Cavaliers frustratingly made no trades at the trade deadline to try to improve the roster. The only notable change the Cavaliers made was moving Hughes to the point guard position and starting Sasha Pavlovic at shooting guard who fit better next to LeBron.

With little roster shakeup from going all in on free agency the year before, the Cavaliers started the year of 12 - 8. The Larry Hughes contract was turning into a disaster as he wasn't a good fit next to LeBron and he wasn't the same player he was in Washington.

LeBron going into year 4 was eligible for a blockbuster 5 year deal. However, he declined the offer and instead signed a 3 year extension with a player option. This ensured he would be a free agent at the same time as draft mates Dwyane Wade and Chris Bosh in 2010 as he did not trust in the front office to build a contender around him in Cleveland.

Year 5

The Cavaliers in this off season did not have alot of cap space to make moves. This was the consequence of their big free agency splash 2 years prior and giving out big contract extensions to players on the decline. Notably Zydrunas Ilgauskas who was starting to decline and Donyell Marshall who was rapidly declining. They had no draft picks for this season. They only notable player they brought in was Devin Brown.

The Cavaliers started off the season rough by mid December their record was 10-14. One of the only bright spots outside of LeBron was the emergence of second year player Daniel Gibson who managed to crack the starting lineup and was a great 3 point shooter. The Cavaliers offensive rating with LeBron was 109 with him on the floor and 96 when he was off the floor.

Alot happened around the mid season mark this time around. LeBron won all star game MVP. The Cavaliers record at this time was 29-23 they were still a good team but worse than prior years due to player decline.

Right before the trade deadline the Cavaliers made a blockbuster move in desperation trading 6 players notably Hughes, and Gooden for 4x Defensive Player of the Year Ben Wallace who was the anchor of the Pistons defense, Delonte West who was a solid scorer, Wally Szczerbiak who was on the decline but still a good 3 point shooter, and lastly Joe Smith who was a solid big man pickup.

After the trade they went 15 - 13 to finish the season finishing with a record of 45-37 finishing as the 4 seed. LeBron led the league in scoring this year averaging a scorching hot 30 points. It took some time for the new additions to figure out how to play with each other but they eventually found some chemistry and got ready for the playoffs.

In the first round once again they matched up against the Washington Wizards. The Cavaliers beat them candidly in 6 games. In the second round they played against the newly formed Boston Celtics Big 3 led by former MVP Kevin Garnett, the do it all scoring threat Paul Pierce, and sharpshooter Ray Allen.

Ultimately the Cavaliers lost in 7 games to the Celtics who would go on to win the championship. The new additions didn't have the impact that the Cavaliers had hoped for. LeBron averaged 26 points and the second highest scorer on his team Ilgauskas averaged 13 points in that series. With two years left on LeBron's contract the front office was on the hot seat to win a championship before it was too late.

Year 6

LeBron put up an incredible performance averaging 38 points 8 rebounds and 8 assists in the series but his supporting cast once again dissapointed against this unique defensive scheme. Mo Williams was outright terrible averaging 18 points but on 37% shooting. They lost in 6 games to the Magic. With 1 year left on LeBron's contract the next season would be do or die for the Cavaliers to win a championship.

In the playoffs Cleveland showed they why they were such an unstoppable force in the regular season. Sweeping the Pistons, and Atlanta Hawks before going on to face the Orlando Magic led by Defensive Player of the Year Dwight Howard. The Magic had excellent perimeter defenders and deployed a unique defensive approach to try and stop the Cavaliers. With LeBron being primarily a slashing player they left him 1 on 1 when he drove into the paint against Dwight Howard.

Joe Smith ended up being bought out and the Cavaliers resigned him that was the only notable pick up that happened by the trade deadline. The Cavaliers ended up finishing the season 66 - 16 which was the best record in the NBA and LeBron won MVP for the first time.

The Mo Williams trade was a smash hit. By December 12th the Cavaliers were 20 - 3. Williams was the co star LeBron had been wanting for years. By the time the all star break came around the Cavaliers were 40 - 11 with both LeBron and Williams being selected as all stars. The MVP debate was very close between LeBron and Kobe being one of the biggest stories the entire year: Who was going to win MVP?

The Cavaliers this season made a huge trade acquiring Mo Williams a 17ppg sharpshooter in his prime while only trading a couple roleplayers away. Unlike Larry Hughes, Williams was a great secondary passer and shooter that in theory would fit perfectly next to LeBron. They also drafted J.J Hickson who was a young athletic big that could finish lobs.

After the loss 4 years prior Team USA was looking to redeem themselves. Lebron, Dwyane Wade , Chris Bosh, Kobe Bryant, and many more star players all came together win gold at the Olympics. Team USA was on a warpath wining every game by a margin of 30 points up until the gold medal game which was close but team USA came out on top. LeBron was at worst 3rd best on the team in every statistical category but led the team in steals, and blocks.

Year 7

To kick off the offseason the Cavaliers traded for Shaquille O'Neal sending out Ben Wallace, and Sasha Pavolic. At 37 years old Shaq wasn't what he used to be but he was still a dominant paint presence being over 300 pounds to compete with the other great bigs in the league. Outside of him they brought in a couple of decent role players like Anthony Parker, and Jamario Moon.

The Cavaliers started off the season slow mainly due to Shaq trying to figure out how to work in the offense. The Cavaliers were significantly worse offensively with Shaq on the floor than off the floor. due to him not being nearly as mobile as he used to be and not having a great mid range game but defensively he could still hold down the paint against other bigs. The Cavaliers record mid December this time around was 18 - 7.

LeBron was the only player on the team selected to the all star game. The front office saw some roster change needed to be made and in desparation before the trade deadline pulled the trigger on acquring Antawn Jamison from the Washington Wizards trading Ilgauskas to to the Wizards and a 1st round pick. He provided spacing with they desperately needed. The Wizards then bought out Ilgauskas contract and the Cavaliers resigned him.

Jamison was on the decline at 33 years old. Him and Shaq conflicted as a duo. Also, Shaq tore a ligament in his finger so they didn't have much time to play with each other and gel. Despite this the Cavaliers once again finished as the best team in the league with a record of 61 - 21. LeBron won his second MVP in a row.

In the first round the Cavaliers took care of the Chicago Bulls in 5 games. In the second round however, they ran into the Big 3 Boston Celtics with breakout star Rajon Rondo. The Cavaliers were no match for the Celtics and lost in 6 games. All of the Cavaliers key players struggled against the Celtics. LeBron had his good games but was terrible in games 5 and 6. In game 5 making 3 of his 14 shots, and in game 6 turning the ball over 9 times.

After the dissapointing end of the season LeBron's contract was up and he was set to become a free agent. Multiple teams flew out to Cleveland to try and convince him to sign with them. On July 8th 2010 LeBron made his announcement during a live ESPN special that would go down as one of the most infamous moments in sports history: The Decision.
